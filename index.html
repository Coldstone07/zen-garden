How We Work: Four Integrated Modalities

Modality 1: Understanding Your Patterns

Why do you keep repeating the same patterns?

Every person has a core fear that drives predictable strategies. The Enneagram maps nine personality structures, each pursuing different rewards to feel safe.

Example: A Type 3 fears worthlessness and pursues achievement to prove their value. A Type 6 fears abandonment and pursues security. Understanding your type explains your triggers, not as character flaws, but as predictable patterns rooted in your core fear.

Why it matters: This depersonalizes your pattern. Instead of self-blame, you can observe your own protective strategy with compassion. You understand HOW you got here.

The Structure of Personality: The Enneagram maps nine distinct personality structures, each with its own core fear, basic desire, and predictable patterns of thinking and behavior.

Three Centers of Intelligence:

  • Head Center (Types 5, 6, 7): Driven by fear—constantly analyzing, trying to predict threat
  • Heart Center (Types 2, 3, 4): Driven by shame—seeking connection, worth, authenticity
  • Body Center (Types 8, 9, 1): Driven by anger—seeking power, peace, or perfection

What Each Type Reveals:

  • Type 1: Core fear of being flawed; pursues perfection and righteousness
  • Type 2: Core fear of being unloved; pursues being needed and valued
  • Type 3: Core fear of worthlessness; pursues achievement and image
  • Type 4: Core fear of being ordinary; pursues authenticity and meaning
  • Type 5: Core fear of incompetence; pursues knowledge and understanding
  • Type 6: Core fear of abandonment; pursues loyalty and security
  • Type 7: Core fear of deprivation; pursues pleasure and stimulation
  • Type 8: Core fear of vulnerability; pursues control and justice
  • Type 9: Core fear of disconnection; pursues peace and harmony

Growth & Stress Directions: When secure, you move toward a healthier type (integration). Under stress, you move toward a less healthy type (disintegration). Understanding your pattern helps you recognize triggers before they control you.

Why This Matters: The Enneagram depersonalizes your pattern. Instead of "I'm broken" or "I'm flawed," you understand: "I'm a Type [X], and this is a predictable protective strategy." That shift—from blame to compassion—is where transformation begins.

In our coaching, we reference your type not to label you, but to help you recognize triggers in real-time. "That reaction activated your core fear" becomes actionable self-knowledge rather than self-judgment.

Modality 2: Accessing Your Core Self

How do you actually transform the pattern?

You're not one unified being—you're a system of "parts," each with good intentions but sometimes misdirected. At your core is the Self, your wise, compassionate center.

Example: Your "Perfectionist" part is trying to protect a younger, wounded part that absorbed the message "You're only safe if you're flawless." The Perfectionist isn't the problem—it's a loyal protector. Once the young part feels safe and releases the old belief, the Perfectionist can relax.

Why it matters: This is how change actually happens. You access your Self-energy (characterized by calm, curiosity, compassion) and from that place, you help your protective parts trust that it's safe to be vulnerable. The nervous system responds to this felt safety.

The Core Insight: You're not one unified being. You're a system of "parts"—distinct sub-personalities, each with its own beliefs, feelings, and agendas. All of them are trying to help, but they're often working at cross-purposes.

The Three Types of Parts:

Managers (Proactive Protectors): These parts work BEFORE a trauma is triggered. Examples: Perfectionist, Inner Critic, People-Pleaser, Over-Achiever. They use control, planning, and vigilance to prevent exiles from being activated. Their strategy: "If I stay in control and meet everyone's needs, nothing bad will happen."

Firefighters (Reactive Protectors): These parts jump in AFTER an exile is triggered. Examples: Rage, Dissociation, Numbing through substances, Procrastination, Compulsive behaviors. Their strategy: "Whatever this painful feeling is, I'll do anything to make it stop—now." They're not the problem; they're an emergency response system.

Exiles (Wounded Parts): Young, vulnerable parts holding old trauma and painful beliefs: "I'm unlovable," "I'm worthless," "It's not safe to be vulnerable." Protectors keep these parts hidden to prevent their pain from overwhelming your system. But their hidden pain is often the fuel for all your reactive patterns.

The Self (Your True Center): Beyond all the parts is your core wisdom—calm, curious, compassionate, confident. The Self is characterized by 8 qualities:

  • Calm: Unshakeable equanimity
  • Curiosity: Genuine interest without judgment
  • Compassion: Deep empathy for yourself and others
  • Confidence: Trust in your wisdom
  • Courage: Willingness to face what's difficult
  • Clarity: Clear seeing without distortion
  • Creativity: Access to new possibilities
  • Connection: Deep attunement to others

The Unblending Process: Most of us are "blended"—fused with our parts. We ARE the anxious part, the angry part, the perfectionist part. IFS teaches you to "unblend"—to step back and observe the part without being consumed by it. From this Self-energy, you can actually dialogue with your parts, understand their good intentions, and help them trust it's safe to relax.

Why This Is the Primary Healing Modality: Talk therapy alone doesn't access the nervous system. Intellectual understanding doesn't change the body's protective responses. But when your Self—characterized by calm, curious compassion—can actually dialogue with exiled parts holding old beliefs, real transformation happens. The parts feel safe enough to release their burdens.

Modality 3: Rewiring Your Nervous System

How does your body learn it's safe to change?

Patterns live in your nervous system, not just your mind. Your body holds trauma and old protective responses. We access this directly through somatic practices, breathwork, and embodied awareness.

Example: That Perfectionist pattern creates chronic chest tension and shallow breathing. Your nervous system learned: "When you relax, bad things happen." Through breathwork and somatic awareness, your body experiences that it's actually SAFE to be calm. Your nervous system rewires.

Why it matters: Intellectual insight alone isn't enough. Your nervous system won't trust your mind's new beliefs. But when your body FEELS safety through regulation, transformation sticks.

The Core Truth: Your body is not separate from your mind—it IS your mind. Trauma, beliefs, and patterns live as much in your nervous system and tissues as they do in your thoughts. This means you can access healing through your body in ways that bypass the thinking mind entirely.

The Three Nervous System States (Polyvagal Theory):

Ventral Vagal (Safe & Social) — The Healing State: Your nervous system is regulated, relaxed, and open. You're connected, creative, compassionate. Your prefrontal cortex (thinking brain) is online. Your Self-energy is accessible. This is where all real transformation happens. Practices: breathwork, safe touch, connection, witnessing.

Sympathetic (Fight/Flight) — The Activated State: Your nervous system is in emergency mode. You're scanning for threat, ready to fight or escape. Your body is flooded with cortisol and adrenaline. Your protective parts are fully activated. You're reactive, defensive, planning. Chronically staying here leads to burnout and anxiety.

Dorsal Vagal (Freeze/Shutdown) — The Collapse State: Your nervous system has given up. You're dissociated, numb, hopeless, disconnected. This is the deepest protective mechanism—when fighting and fleeing feel impossible. Many people oscillate between Sympathetic and Dorsal Vagal, never accessing Ventral Vagal safety.

Interoception — Learning to Feel from Within: Most of us have learned to dissociate from our bodies as a survival strategy. We ignore hunger, pain, emotion, and intuition. Somatic training teaches you to turn your awareness inward and notice subtle sensations—the tightness in your chest when anxious, the warmth in your belly when safe, the contraction in your shoulders before you snap.

Theta State Access: Deep healing often requires accessing your subconscious mind (4-8 Hz brainwave state). We use guided somatic practices, breathwork, and meditation to facilitate this state where profound rewiring becomes possible—much faster than talking about your patterns.

Equanimity to Sensation: Rather than pushing sensations away or getting consumed by them, you learn to witness them with curious, compassionate awareness. "Interesting, there's tightness in my throat. What's it protecting against? What does it need?" This non-reactivity to sensation fundamentally changes your relationship with emotion and pain.

Why Body-Based Healing is Essential: Your nervous system doesn't believe intellectual insights. But when your body EXPERIENCES safety—through regulated co-presence with a coach, through interoceptive awareness, through somatic practices—your nervous system learns to trust at a cellular level. This is why transformation through somatic work sticks.
