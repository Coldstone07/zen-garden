Why It Matters: Theta is where healing happens—deeper than conscious thought.

How We Use It: Guided meditations and somatic practices specifically designed to access Theta states

Basal Cognition (Michael Levin)

What It Is: Emerging field showing intelligence at the cellular level; traumas are groups of cells separated from the higher self/whole.

Why It Matters: Explains why our somatic approach works—we leverage the system's own intelligence rather than trying to force change.

How We Use It: Trust the body's intelligence; somatic release; cellular reintegration

Network & Attachment Theory

What It Is: Neural networks become rigid and isolated from trauma; healing increases connectivity and flexibility. Secure attachment repatterns internal working models.

Why It Matters: Explains how trauma fragments the self and how compassionate Self-relationship heals it.

How We Use It: IFS Self-relationship as secure internal attachment; neural repattering through compassionate presence

Polyvagal Theory (Dr. Stephen Porges)

What It Is: The vagus nerve's role in nervous system regulation; "ventral vagal" = calm/connected, "sympathetic" = alert/threat, "dorsal vagal" = shutdown.

Why It Matters: Shows how self-compassion physically regulates the nervous system.

How We Use It: Self-energy as "ventral vagal anchor"; somatic techniques activate parasympathetic response
